## Chapter IV-b — The Search

**POV:** First — Avery
**Script pages:** 29–39
**Tone:** Warmth cracking. Nathaniel's fear takes the shape of anger. Avery doesn't understand why.
**Arc:** She hears something that can't be unhearned → collapses → the absurdity of the morning oath holds her together

### Scene 1 — `nathaniel-storms-in`

Nathaniel comes home and goes directly to Avery's room. She's not there. He goes through everything.

**Exact dialogue (script p.29):**
- *"WHERE IS THAT BOOK? WHERE IS IT??"*
- Ryllae: *"What is this?? What are you looking for?"*
- Nathaniel: *"That book!! I need that book!!"*
- Ryllae: *"Don't raise your voice at me. What book? What's the matter?"*
- Nathaniel: *"She shouldn't be reading that book, Mom!! She shouldn't learn all of this! Especially a child like her!!"*
- Ryllae: *"She's not a child anymore. She's very mature, you know."*
- Nathaniel: *"That is precisely the problem!"*

**Spots:**
- "The Book" — what he's afraid she'll learn; not the book itself, the path it opens; knowing what she can do and having a name for it are two different things
- "A Child Like Her" — she will replay that phrase for days; she tries to hold it at arm's length; it won't stay there

**Image:** `no-nathaniel-storms.jpeg`
**Prose note:** Avery isn't present — she returns to find the room already searched. The mess of it is what lands.

---

### Scene 2 — `avery-crying`

Avery finds Ryllae. She's been crying without noise into her mother's shoulder. The phrase "a child like her" is still in the room.

**Exact dialogue (script p.31–32):**
- Avery: *"Why am I always like this mom? I don't want to be like this. Why…?"*
- Ryllae: *"What are you talking about, my dear?"*
- Avery: *"I don't know… I just… sometimes I feel like I'm the problem. Why do I always cause trouble, mom?"*
- Ryllae: *"You don't cause trouble. You are exactly who you are meant to be."*

**Spots:**
- "What She Wants to Say" — she almost says *I wish I were normal*; she stops because she doesn't quite believe it; that's the worst part
- "Ryllae's Answer" — she didn't say *everything's fine*; she said something different; Avery will hold that for a while

**Image:** `no-avery-crying.jpeg`
**Prose note:** No filter words. Show: her hands on Ryllae's sleeve. The specific quality of crying that has no sound.

---

### Scene 3 — `the-school-bag`

Nathaniel, still searching, finds a small pink bag under Avery's bed. He goes still.

**Exact beat (script p.35):**
*"oh god… no, no way... No way!"*

The pink Eiffel Tower school bag. It's just a child's bag. The sight of it breaks something. He sits on the edge of the bed. Ryllae stands in the doorway.

**Spots:**
- "The Pink Bag" — what he was afraid he'd find, and what he found instead; the gap between those two things; the dissonance doesn't fix anything, but it changes the shape of it
- "What He Says After" — nothing; he sets it down and leaves the room; Ryllae watches him go

**Image:** `no-school-bag.jpeg`
**Prose note:** This is Nathaniel's scene but told from Avery's POV — she finds the bag back on the bed, not under it. She knows what that means.

---

### Scene 4 — `morning-walk`

Next morning. Avery and Callen walk to school. She needs a promise.

**Exact dialogue (script p.37–38):**
- Avery: *"Swear to the River Goddess you won't say anything to anyone."*
- Callen: *"I swear to the River Goddess."*
- Avery, flat: *"That's exactly how you swear, you have to say it right."*
- Callen: *"I swear by the River Goddess that I will not say anything to anyone about what has happened."*
- Avery: *"Or else?"*
- Callen: *"Or else she will curse me."*
- Avery: *"And."*
- Callen: *"...And I will be forced to run naked across the village square during the Blue River Festival."*

**Spots:**
- "The Oath" — she invented the terms; she needed to hear them said aloud more than she needed the actual promise; the absurdity is the relief
- "Why This Morning Matters" — she will remember the walk, not the night before; the walk is the thing she chooses to keep

**Image:** `no-morning-walk-school.jpeg`
**Prose note:** The oath is funny. Avery lets it be funny. That's the whole scene.

## Chapter V-a — Festival Day

**POV:** First — Avery
**Script pages:** 39–43
**Tone:** School is social armor. The classroom is the one place she forgets to be anxious. Kathrine takes that too.
**Arc:** The one safe room; Kathrine poisons it; Jane hands her a list and the warmth comes back

### Scene 1 — `chief-kain`

Morning. Village square. Chief Kain (Kain Alvertos — thick beard, prominent eyebrows, sharp eyes, gallant bearing) stops Nathaniel at the door. Quietly. Nathaniel goes back inside.

Avery and Callen watch from across the square. She doesn't hear what was said. She keeps walking.

Kathrine at the gate: *"What have you done to him? Did you poison him?"*
Avery: *"I didn't do anything."*
Kathrine, walking ahead: *"Right."*

**Spots:**
- "Chief Kain" — what he said; player infers: stand down, let it settle; the village still believes in this family; Nathaniel heard that
- "Poison Him" — loud enough for two students to hear; Kathrine doesn't say things accidentally

**Image:** `no-chief-kain.jpeg`

---

### Scene 2 — `professor-milos`

Classroom. Long wooden tables. Professor Milos at the front.

**Professor Milos:** messy hair with natural upward spikes like feathers, a beard that has grown without a plan, round spectacles perpetually tilted, a long academic robe washed many times, ink stains on one sleeve. The kind of person who starts a sentence and remembers something better halfway through. His class is the one place she forgets to track herself.

Mid-lesson: Festival Day announcement, but he extends the River Goddess story first.

**Exact dialogue (script p.40–42):**
- Milos: *"The River Goddess blessed the Blue River, and those who came to live on its banks did so under her protection. Or so the story goes."* (beat) *"The Festival is tomorrow. Who can tell me what the ritual means?"*
- Kathrine, half-raising her hand: *"It's asking the goddess to ignore us for another year."*
- Class laughs.
- Kathrine, directly at Avery: *"Some of us probably shouldn't bother. She clearly didn't bless everyone."*
- Avery looks at the table.

**Spots:**
- "Professor Milos" — who he is; his class is the only place she forgets to be anxious; she hadn't named that until now; the forgetting was the point
- "The Goddess Story" — what the ritual actually means; what Avery believes about it; she has never said; she's not sure she's allowed to believe it

**Image:** `no-professor-milos.jpeg`

---

### Scene 3 — `festival-announced`

After Milos's lesson. The class is planning. Jane already has a list — choral rehearsal, timing, positions, exact note.

- Callen, leaning over: *"Is she always like this?"*
- Avery: *"Since she was seven."*

Jane's list is in her hand. She's reading it. A moment ago she was dreading tomorrow.

**Spot:**
- "Jane's Lists" — the joy is in the system; Jane isn't anxious, she's organized; Avery has wanted to understand the difference since they were children; she still doesn't, but she holds the list anyway

**Image:** `no-festival-announced.jpeg`

## Chapter V-b — The Heist

**POV:** First — Avery
**Script pages:** 43–63
**Tone:** Two halves. First: comedy with real stakes and real loyalty. Second: the walk — first time Kathrine and Avery are alone; the green crystal; something shifts.
**Arc:** Challenge accepted → disaster → Jane saves it → Kathrine and Avery alone → green crystal given → Bram falls in mud

### Scene 1 — `after-school`

After school. The group at the usual corner. Silas has information: something near Bronzefinger's, a window left open.

Kathrine, to Avery directly: *"She has to do this. If you want to be one of us, prove it."*
Avery: *"Prove it how."*
Kathrine: *"Take something from Bronzefinger's forge. Bring it back. If you can do it, you're one of us. If you can't..."* She shrugs.

**Spots:**
- "The Challenge" — Kathrine has never invited Avery into anything before; the framing is cruel; the fact of the invitation is not; Avery holds both things at once
- "If You Can't" — Avery decided before Kathrine finished the sentence; she's not sure what she decided but she decided

**Image:** `no-after-school.jpeg`

---

### Scene 2 — `the-distraction`

**Bronzefinger:** large, broad, thick around the middle, slicked-back grey hair, spectacular handlebar mustache, heavy square chin, thick low eyebrows, small fierce eyes. His shop runs exactly the way he wants it.

Callen's plan: walk in the front. Be loud. Be Callen.

**Exact dialogue (script p.46):**
*"Mr. Bronzefinger! Best Friend! BF! That stands for two things today!"*

Bronzefinger: not angry. Vaguely curious where this is going.

Avery, through the side: sword rack. She takes one. Heavier than expected. She trips. Sword clangs. Bronzefinger turns.

**Spots:**
- "BF" — the joke works not because Bronzefinger found it funny, but because it took him a second to process the audacity; that second is everything
- "The Weight of It" — she assumed she could carry it; she cannot; this is embarrassing and she will not say so

**Image:** `no-the-distraction.jpeg`

---

### Scene 3 — `jane-saves-them`

Sword on the ground. Bronzefinger walking toward Avery.

Jane, in the doorway: *"My dad says he really needs the tools you promised him. He said it's urgent."*

Bronzefinger stops. Looks at Jane. Looks at Avery. Looks at the sword.

Avery puts it back. They leave.

Outside: Kathrine checks Callen's jacket. The hammer is there. He took it himself, quietly, while everyone watched Avery fall. He said nothing.

**Spots:**
- "Jane's Timing" — she appeared too fast for coincidence; she was watching the whole time; she had a plan; the plan was Avery
- "Callen's Hammer" — he didn't announce it; he just did it; the hammer is the answer to the challenge; he understood the challenge before Avery did

**Image:** `no-jane-saves.jpeg`

---

### Scene 4 — `the-walk`

Walking toward the cave. The boys go ahead. Kathrine falls into step beside Avery. First time they've walked anywhere alone.

**Exact dialogue (script p.56–60):**
- Kathrine: *"I'm a bit surprised you would take my challenge."*
- Avery: *"Why?"*
- Kathrine: *"I didn't think you'd actually do it."*
- Avery: *"Then why did you ask?"*
- Kathrine doesn't answer that.
- Kathrine: *"Callen told me."* (beat) *"About the crystals. About what you can do."*
- Avery: *"When?"*
- Kathrine: *"Does it matter?"*
- (long pause)
- Kathrine: *"Can you make one now?"*
- Avery reaches for a handful of leaves. Finds the pull — quieter than the river pull. Green crystal forms in her palm.
- Kathrine takes it.
- Kathrine: *"It's warm."*
- Avery: *"They're always warm at first."*
- Kathrine pockets it. That's all.

**Spots:**
- "Why She Asked" — Kathrine said *I didn't think you'd do it*, not *I wanted you to fail*; those are different sentences; Avery will think about this for days
- "The Green Crystal" — she's never made one from leaves before; she didn't know she could; she won't say that; she just holds it out

**Image:** `no-the-walk.jpeg`
**Canon note:** Kathrine keeps the green crystal. She has it at the Festival night. She may have it during the assault — not verified, don't invent.

---

### Scene 5 — `waterfall`

The path passes a waterfall. The boys stop. Silas says something to Bram. Bram, looking at the water, takes one step closer.

He falls. Mud. Complete.

Everyone still. Then Callen has to sit down.

**Exact dialogue (script p.62):**
Silas: *"I just said you were looking at Avery."*
Bram, from the mud: silence.

**Spot:**
- "Bram in the Mud" — she doesn't know what to do with her face; she looks at the waterfall for a long time; this is also filed under *think about this later*

**Image:** `no-waterfall.jpeg`
**Prose note:** Brief. Let it breathe. It's funny. Don't explain it.

## Chapter V-c — The Blue River Festival

**POV:** First — Avery
**Script source:** Implied throughout; festival night precedes the assault; the warmth is the last warmth
**Tone:** Full warmth. Music. The whole village. Nothing has broken yet.
**Arc:** The ritual → the river moment → five of them on the grass, everything still whole

### Scene 1 — `the-square-lit`

Night. The square transformed. Lanterns on every post and windowsill. The river lit below.

Jane's choir on the raised platform. She organized it, directed it, is now refusing to admit she's nervous. The song: a traditional verse about the goddess. Three-part harmony. Jane's voice carries.

**Spots:**
- "The Song" — the words; an old prayer that stopped being a prayer and became a song; some people still mean it; she doesn't know which she is
- "What the Light Does" — the river and the lanterns at once; the village has looked like this every year for eighty years; she knows that; it doesn't make it less

**Image:** `no-festival-square.jpeg`

---

### Scene 2 — `the-procession`

The ritual walk to the river's edge. Offerings. Flowers floated on the current. They drift south.

Avery at the back. Crystal in her pocket. She takes it out — not to offer it. Just to hold it over the water.

The river recognizes it. The warmth moves through.

**Spots:**
- "The Offering" — she didn't float it; she almost did; she put it back; she doesn't know what that means
- "Recognition" — the warmth that comes up through the crystal into her hand; the river has done this since the first time; it hasn't become ordinary

**Image:** `no-the-procession.jpeg`

---

### Scene 3 — `the-group-after`

After the ritual. The five of them on the grass near the river. Silas lying down. Bram (cleaned up) throwing small stones into the current. Jane still humming. Callen asleep or pretending.

Avery watches the village.

Kathrine sits down beside her. Not talking. Just there.

Green crystal in Kathrine's hand — she has it out, turning it in her fingers.

**Spots:**
- "What She Holds" — she has never sat near Kathrine without tracking where the exit is; she's not tracking now; she doesn't have a word for this yet
- "Before Dawn" — she had this same feeling coming out of the cave; the village ahead, still lit; this time she doesn't want to go inside; she wants to hold it still

**Image:** `no-group-after.jpeg`
**Prose note:** Final scene of this chapter links forward — next is the assault. The warmth here must be complete. Nothing foreboding. Let the reader carry it.

## Writing Notes for New Chapters

### Kathrine arc through these chapters
- IV-b: she's absent; her cruelty from Ch II is still in the room
- V-a: she poisons the classroom; loud enough for witnesses
- V-b first half: she issues the challenge; this is control
- V-b second half: she walks beside Avery; she asks; she takes the crystal; this is something else
- V-c: she sits down next to Avery without explanation; green crystal in her hand

The arc is: cruelty → public cruelty → control → curiosity → presence. Don't collapse it.

### The crystal colors
- Amber: the river crystal; the first one; always warm; irreplaceable
- Clear: made without the river (demonstrated in Ch IV)
- White/cold: panic crystal (Ch VI — the freeze)
- Green: leaves, Kathrine's crystal (Ch V-b)
Each crystal has a different pull. Avery knows this. The reader learns it through her noticing.

### Prose temperature by chapter
- IV-b: warm interior light going uncertain; not cold yet
- V-a: school warm + social cold coexisting
- V-b: comedy warmth → late afternoon warmth → forest quiet
- V-c: full warmth, no cracks; this is the last time

### Filter word checklist (all new prose)
- No: felt, saw, heard, noticed, thought, realized, seemed, watched, looked like
- No emotion labels: not "she was afraid" → show the hands, the counting, the thing she fixes her eyes on
- Speech tags: only `said` and `asked`
- Sentences: one thing each
